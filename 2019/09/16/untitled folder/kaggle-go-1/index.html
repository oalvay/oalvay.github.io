Kaggle Competition -- What I've learned from it

The experience and knowledge I gained from Kaggle competition, about Exploratory Data Analysis, Featurer Engineering and more.

I joined the competition a month ago. For a considerable proportion of time, I blindly copied codes from public kernels and discussions, not to learn but seeked for a tiny increase in Publice Score to move my ranking forwards.

I realized that I was on the very wrong way to learn practical machine learning, so I am now starting all over again, bulid everying from very bottom to the top, and for everying I did, I need to understand at least one of the following:

  • How?
    — The reason or theory behind it.
  • Why?
    — How can it make prediction better.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Aiming

There are many types of data analysis, depend on aiming. Here, what we want is the information that helps us to make a better prediction.

Graphs

The best choice for continuous variable and time series data

  • Univariate
    Histogram is the one that easiest to illustrate and most intuitive. For a single variable, it is best to draw histogram twice on same graph, one on train set and one on test set. So we can not only see the structure of feature nut also detect difference between train and test set to prevent potential overfitting. There are several ways to do so, including:

  • One hot Encoding
  • Label Encoding
